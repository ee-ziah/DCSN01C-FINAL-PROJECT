Yokohama Yokohama Yokohama

Yokohama, Japan's second-largest city, offers a refreshing contrast to Tokyo's intensity while maintaining its own sophisticated charm. Just a short train ride from central Tokyo, the city unfolds along a scenic waterfront that speaks to its history as one of Japan's first ports opened to foreign trade. The heart of the city is its striking harbor area, Minato Mirai 21, where the iconic Landmark Tower (once Japan's tallest building) stands alongside the red-brick warehouses of Aka-renga. These historic buildings have been transformed into a stylish complex of shops, restaurants, and event spaces that capture both the city's maritime past and contemporary culture.
