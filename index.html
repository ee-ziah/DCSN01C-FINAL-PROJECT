Shinjuku Shinjuku Shinjuku

Shinjuku is a frenetic heart of modern Tokyo, where towering skyscrapers cast long shadows over narrow alleys packed with tiny izakayas and ramen shops. The world's busiest train station serves as its nexus, with over 3.5 million people passing through daily in a remarkably orderly chaos. By day, suited office workers stream through the commercial district anchored by the imposing Metropolitan Government Building. But as night falls, the eastern side transforms – particularly in Kabukicho, where neon signs bathe the streets in electric colors and touts beckon passersby into karaoke bars and restaurants. The district holds surprising pockets of tranquility too. The Gyoen National Garden offers a peaceful respite with its traditional Japanese landscape gardens and greenhouse. Meanwhile, the Golden Gai's network of six narrow alleys houses over 200 tiny bars, each seating just a handful of patrons – a reminder of old Tokyo persisting within the modern metropolis.
